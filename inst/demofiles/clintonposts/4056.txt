<p class="MsoNormal"><a href="http://campusprogress.org/">Campus Progress</a>, affiliate of the <a href="http://www.americanprogress.org/">Center for American Progress</a>, will have their annual <a href="http://campusprogress.org/common/801/the-2006-campus-progress-national-student-conference">National Student Conference</a> next Wednesday, July 12 in Washington, D.C.. The <a href="http://campusprogress.org/common/908/2006-campus-progress-national-student-conference-agenda.">list of speakers</a> is impressive, and includes Senator Barak Obama, former Clinton Chief of Staff John Podesta, and Pulitzer Prize-winning author <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0060541644/sr=8-1/qid=1152142557/ref=pd_bbs_1/002-8741085-4743210?ie=UTF8">Samantha Powers</a>, among others. Panel topics include skills seminar (on topics like advocacy writing) and issue discussions on poverty, international intervention, and academic freedom, among others.</p>    <p class="MsoNormal">The conference is free, but the registration deadline was back on June 15 and Campus Progress has met their registration goals. However, reportedly they are still accepting registrations for a limited time. Go <a href="http://campusprogress.org/page/s/2006app">here to sign up</a>, and I hope to see you there. </p>
