<p>As everyone must have heard by now, <strong><a href="http://www.newhavenindependent.org/HealthCare/archives/2006/04/lamont_calls_fo.html">universal health care proponant</a>, <a href="http://nedlamont.com/issues/553/marriage-equality">marriage equality supporter</a>, and <a href="http://www.democracynow.org/article.pl?sid=06/08/08/1453227">Iraq war foe</a> <a href="http://www.newhavenindependent.org/archives/2006/08/lamont_crushes_1.php">Ned Lamont beat Joe Lieberman in the Ct. Democratic primary</a></strong>. The <strong><a href="http://www.thenation.com/doc/20060814/melber">progressive blogosphere</a></strong> is showing it&#8217;s muscle with folks like <strong><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/storyonly/2006/8/9/41720/34915">KOS</a></strong>, <strong><a href="http://www.mydd.com/tag/Ned%20Lamont">MYDD</a></strong>, and others leading the way.</p>
<p>Democrats such as <strong><a href="http://www.rawstory.com/news/2006/Boxers_office_says_campaigning_for_Lamont_0809.html">Barbara Boxer</a></strong>, <a href="http://www.swingstateproject.com/2006/08/ctsen_schumer_a.php"><strong>Harry Ried</strong>, <strong>Chuck Schumer</strong></a>, <strong><a href="http://hotlineblog.nationaljournal.com/archives/2006/08/post_50.html">Barack Obama</a></strong>, <strong><a href="http://www.madison.com/tct/opinion/column/nichols/index.php?ntid=93793">Russ Feingold</a></strong>, <a href="http://hotlineblog.nationaljournal.com/archives/2006/08/post_50.html">and others</a> have stood up to back <a href="http://lamontblog.blogspot.com/">Ned Lamont</a> in the general election. <strong><a href="http://www.blogforamerica.com/archives/008116.html">Progressive firebrand Maxine Waters</a> <a href="http://www.newhavenindependent.org/archives/2006/08/waters_pays_tri.php">gave the blogosphere credit where credit is due</a></strong>. <a href="http://www.inthesetimes.com/site/main/article/2549/">Lamont</a> of course had the courage to go <a href="http://www.rawstory.com/news/2006/Senate_candidate_Ned_Lamont_appears_on_0801.html">head to head with Stephen Colbert</a>.</p>
<p>As a Jewish American, I must say I&#8217;m thrilled. There are plenty of other Jews in government who hold reasonable and even great positions on most topics (<a href="http://lautenberg.senate.gov/"><strong>Frank Lautenberg</strong> D-NJ</a>, <a href="http://www.barneyfrank.net/"><strong>Barney Frank</strong> D-MA</a>, and of course the excellent<strong> <a href="http://www.russfeingold.org/">Senator Russ Feingold</a></strong> D-WI come to mind).</p>
<p>Progressives such as <a href="http://www.madison.com/tct/mad/topstories/index.php?ntid=94136&#038;ntpid=0"><strong>Russ Feingold</strong></a> (the first Senate Dem to refuse to endores Lieberman) are <a href="http://www.thenation.com/blogs/thebeat?bid=1&#038;pid=111660">viewing this as a sign that progressive positions are gaining traction</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Lamont&#8217;s win bodes well for Progressives and challengers in the next election cycle</strong>, and is great news for Bob Casey in his bid to overthrow a conservative incumbant.</p>
